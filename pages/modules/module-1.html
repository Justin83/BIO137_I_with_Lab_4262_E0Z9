Why It Matters

Unit 1 introduces the study of human anatomy and physiology that builds a foundation for everything else you will learn about the human body in this class and beyond. The terminology in Chapter 1 will be used over and over again as you study each organ system. The basic chemistry covered in Chapter 2 is essential for your understanding of the function of the muscular, nervous, cardiovascular, urinary, respiratory, and digestive systems, and more!

The concepts presented in Chapter 3 will help you appreciate how the cells in each organ system carry out the work that is essential for that system to function and to maintain the health of the body. The work you put into studying the concepts in Module 1 will make your future studies more meaningful and help you appreciate the extraordinary complexity of the human body. Happy studying!

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to demonstrate competency in the following:

1

Compare and contrast anatomy and physiology.

2

Use correct terms to describe body organization, relative position, body regions, and directional relationships.

3

Explain basic principles of chemistry.

4

Describe cell structure and function.

How to Navigate This Module

Be sure to complete the assignments in the order listed! This module covers three chapters and three labs. Begin each chapter by reading through that chapter on your own using the digital e-text or, if you prefer, the optional hard copy you purchased. Once you are familiar with the chapter, you can complete the assignments more effectively.

Within each module, you will find an interactive reading assignment and quiz over each chapter, three lab exercises (some labs have two parts) and three lab quizzes, one Case Study assignment, one Lecture Exam, and one Lab Exam. Some modules also include videos to support your studies.
