Introduction

Welcome to BIO 137 online lecture and lab/Human Anatomy & Physiology! This course is the first semester in a two-semester sequence that covers the structures and function of the human body. This semester we will study basic chemistry, cell structure, cell function, tissues, and the integumentary, skeletal, muscular, and nervous systems.

In this section, you will learn how the course is organized, how to navigate the course, and how to contact your instructor. You will also learn about important course policies.

Why it matters: This section covers important information about the course that you can put to immediate use.

How you’ll use this: You will use this information to navigate through the course, find work, and submit it.

Modules

Each module includes the following:

Module
Module Overview — key components of the module and what you are expected to do.
3 Chapters — study materials, Interactive SmartBook Exercise, and Chapter Practice Quiz for each chapter.
3 Lab Topics — assigned Lab Exercise activity or activities and the corresponding Lab Quiz for each lab topic. Some lab exercises may include more than one part, but each lab topic counts as one lab exercise grade and one lab quiz grade.
Case Study Assignment — application of chapter concepts to a practical, real-life situation.
Exams — one lecture exam over all three chapters and one lab exam over all three lab topics.
Give Feedback — an opportunity to provide feedback about your experience in the module.

Finding Work in the Class

Once you open a module, go through each page in order by selecting the arrow at the right portion of the module. You can also use the arrow to the left to go to a previous page in the module.

Each module includes information and activities that will help you reach the objectives for the module. It is crucial that you go through each page in a module because many pages offer explanation for an assignment before you complete it. Do not make the mistake of only following the course schedule and trying to complete only the graded activities.

You are required to access all course materials through Blackboard. Begin and end all assignments in Blackboard. Some assignments will transfer you to other programs, but when you complete an assignment, choose the option to “return to Blackboard” to help make sure your grades transfer properly.

Online Etiquette

This video will provide an overview of online etiquette, including guidelines and good practices for online communication in emails, discussion forums, and chat rooms.

Please practice the following guidelines when posting comments in our course:

  • Format your text so it is easy to read. Use standard paragraph spacing, indentation, and legible font choices.
  • Be concise and to the point in your communication. Limit discussion forum posts to a few paragraphs and proofread before posting.
  • Avoid the use of all caps. All caps may be interpreted as shouting and can make text more difficult to read.
  • Avoid abbreviations such as OMG.
  • Avoid humor and sarcasm when it may be difficult to detect tone in written communication.
  • Flaming is not proper in our courses. Practice civil discussion.
  • Respect the privacy of communication within a course. Do not publicly forward classmates’ comments outside of the course.
  • Stay on topic in discussions.
  • Be respectful. Share comments in ways that are not likely to be misinterpreted or provide constructive suggestions privately when appropriate.
