Meet Your Instructor

Welcome to BIO 137 Human Anatomy and Physiology I. My name is Justin Howard, and I will be your instructor for this course. This is an online, accelerated summer course, so the pace will be steady. My goal is to help you stay organized, understand the material, and know how to get help before small problems become big ones.

Anatomy and physiology can feel like a new language at first. That is normal. You will be learning body regions, directional terms, chemistry, cell structure, tissues, and organ systems. The students who do best usually work consistently, read before attempting assignments, use the practice opportunities, and contact me early when they have questions.

Best Way to Reach Me

The best way to contact me for most course questions is through Blackboard Messages or KCTCS email. Use Blackboard Messages when your question is connected to the course, an assignment, a grade, or course navigation. Email is also fine, especially if you are having trouble accessing Blackboard.

You should generally receive a response from me within 48 hours, not counting weekends, holidays, or college closures. If you do not hear back within that time, please send your message again.

If your question is urgent or would be easier to talk through, you may also call my office phone at 606-451-6993. I am also available through Microsoft Teams when a live conversation or screen sharing would be helpful.

Need live help? Message me in Blackboard or email me to request a phone call or Microsoft Teams meeting. Teams is especially helpful when you are stuck in Blackboard, Connect, an assignment, or a lab and need us to look at the same screen together.

My Role as Your Instructor

My role is to provide guidance, structure, feedback, and support as you work through the course. I will help clarify expectations, explain difficult concepts, answer questions, and help you make a plan when you are struggling.

I cannot complete the work for you, but I can help you understand what the work is asking, how to approach the material, and how to use the tools available in Blackboard and Connect.

Technical Support

I can help with basic course navigation and assignment expectations. For technical problems beyond my control, such as password issues, Blackboard access errors, browser problems, Connect access issues, or system errors, I may direct you to KCTCS, Blackboard, or McGraw Hill technical support.

Grading and Feedback Policy

Grading Response Time

Many assignments in this course are graded automatically and will show scores quickly. Assignments that require instructor review will usually receive feedback within one week after the due date, unless there are extenuating circumstances or a large number of submissions to review.

If something appears to be missing or ungraded after a reasonable amount of time, please contact me so I can check it.

Finding Grades in Blackboard Ultra

In Blackboard Ultra, use the gradebook or Grades area to review submitted work and posted grades. You should check your grades regularly, especially after major course deadlines.

Do not rely only on the percentage shown in Blackboard. To understand your standing in the course, divide the points you have earned by the number of points possible at that point in the semester.

Grading Criteria

This course uses a total-points grading system. A total of 1405 points can be earned through the assignments, quizzes, labs, exams, and final exam described in the course syllabus and schedule.

  • To earn an A, students must have 1258 or more points. This is equivalent to 89.5% or above.
  • To earn a B, students must have 1117 to 1257 points. This is equivalent to 79.5 to 89.4%.
  • To earn a C, students must have 977 to 1116 points. This is equivalent to 69.5 to 79.4%.
  • To earn a D, students must have 836 to 976 points. This is equivalent to 59.5 to 69.4%.
  • To earn an E, students must earn 835 or fewer points. This is equivalent to below 59.5%.

Determining Your Grade During the Semester

After each major deadline, evaluate your progress by dividing the total points you have earned by the total number of points possible at that point. This gives you a more accurate picture of your course standing than the Blackboard percentage alone.

A detailed list of assignments, point values, topics, and due dates can be found in the course schedule and assignment list. Contact me if you are unsure how to interpret your grade.

Learner Participation and Expectations

This is an online, accelerated 8-week course. You are expected to log in often, read announcements, check Blackboard Messages and KCTCS email, and complete work by the posted deadlines. Course access and assignment submissions are tracked in Blackboard and Connect.

Attendance in this online course is based on completion of assigned work. Simply opening Blackboard does not replace completing assignments, quizzes, labs, or exams. The Start Here assignments are especially important because they are used as evidence of attendance during the first week of the course.

Most weeks include reading, SmartBook or interactive assignments, chapter practice quizzes, lab exercises, lab quizzes, and occasional case study work. Exam weeks may also include regular weekly assignments.

Learner Feedback

At the end of modules or at selected points in the course, you may have opportunities to provide feedback about the course. Blackboard or the college may also prompt you to complete a course survey. Your feedback helps me identify what is working, what needs clarification, and where students may need additional support.
