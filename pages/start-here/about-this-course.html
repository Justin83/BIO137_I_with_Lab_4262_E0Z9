Introduction

Welcome to BIO 137: Human Anatomy & Physiology I with Lab. This online course is the first semester in a two-semester sequence that covers the structure and function of the human body. This semester we will study basic chemistry, cell structure, cell function, tissues, and the integumentary, skeletal, muscular, and nervous systems.

In this section, you will learn how the course is organized, how to navigate the course, and how to contact me. You will also review several important course policies.

Why it matters: This section covers important information about the course that you can put to immediate use.

How you’ll use this: You will use this information to navigate the course, find work, and submit it.

Modules

Each module follows a consistent pattern so that you know what to expect as you move through the course.

Module
Module Overview — key components of the module and what you are expected to complete.
3 Chapters — study materials, Interactive SmartBook Exercises, and Chapter Practice Quizzes for the assigned textbook chapters.
3 Lab Topics — assigned Lab Exercise activity or activities and the corresponding Lab Quiz for each lab topic. Some lab exercises may include more than one part, but each lab topic counts as one lab exercise grade and one lab quiz grade.
Case Study Assignment — application of chapter concepts to a practical or real-life situation.
Module Exams — one lecture exam over the assigned chapters and one lab exam over the assigned lab topics.
Give Feedback — a brief feedback opportunity that may appear at the end of a module but is separate from the main graded coursework.

Finding Work in the Class

Once you open a module, move through the pages in order by selecting the arrow on the right side of the module page. You can also use the arrow on the left side to return to a previous page.

Each module includes information and activities that will help you reach the objectives for the module. It is important to go through each page in a module because many pages explain an assignment before you complete it. Do not rely only on the course schedule and skip directly to the graded activities.

You are required to access all course materials through Blackboard. Begin and end all assignments in Blackboard. Some assignments will transfer you to other programs, such as McGraw Hill Connect, but when you complete an assignment, choose the option to return to Blackboard when available. This helps make sure your grades transfer properly.

Online Etiquette

This video provides an overview of online etiquette, including good practices for online communication in messages, discussion forums, and virtual meetings.

Please practice the following guidelines when posting comments or communicating in the course:

  • Format your text so it is easy to read. Use standard paragraph spacing and legible font choices.
  • Be concise and to the point. Keep discussion posts focused and proofread before posting.
  • Avoid the use of all caps. All caps may be interpreted as shouting and can make text more difficult to read.
  • Avoid informal abbreviations such as OMG in academic course communication.
  • Avoid humor and sarcasm when tone may be difficult to detect in written communication.
  • Practice civil discussion. Flaming, insults, or hostile comments are not appropriate in this course.
  • Respect the privacy of communication within the course. Do not publicly forward classmates’ comments outside of the course.
  • Stay on topic in discussions and course messages.
  • Be respectful. Share comments in ways that are not likely to be misinterpreted, and provide constructive suggestions privately when appropriate.
