Course Description

BIO 137 Human Anatomy and Physiology I is an introduction to human anatomy and physiology. This course studies the interrelationship of structure and function of each body system across two semesters. The first semester includes basic chemistry, cell structure, cell physiology, metabolism, tissues, and the integumentary, skeletal, muscular, and nervous systems.

The laboratory portion corresponds to lecture and uses anatomical models, computer programs, charts, slides of microscopic tissue sections, physiological experiments, and preserved specimens for dissection to reinforce principles and structures discussed in lecture.

Assignments

SmartBook Exercises

Twelve SmartBook exercises are worth 20 points each. They have no time limit and may be repeated before the due date.

Chapter Practice Quizzes

Twelve quizzes are worth 20 points each. Quizzes are open-book/open-note, limited to 30 minutes, may be repeated up to 3 times, and use the best score.

Case Studies, Lab Exercises, and Lab Quizzes

Four case studies are submitted in Blackboard and are worth 20 points each. Twelve lab exercises are worth 12 points each and may be repeated before the due date. Twelve lab quizzes are worth 12 points each, limited to 30 minutes, and may be repeated up to 3 times.

Exams

Module Exams

  • Four module exams at the end of each 3-chapter module.
  • Each module includes separate lecture and lab exams.
  • Lecture: 30 questions, 60 points, 60 minutes.
  • Lab: 20 questions, 40 points, 40 minutes.
  • Combined module value: 100 points.

Final Exam

  • Comprehensive over all 12 lecture chapters and all 12 lab activities.
  • Lecture: 60 questions, 60 points, 120 minutes.
  • Lab: 40 questions, 40 points, 80 minutes.
  • Total final value: 100 points.

Final review quizzes are worth 20 points total. Exams open according to the Blackboard schedule and cannot be repeated for higher scores.

One Free Missed Module Exam: Each student has one free missed module exam opportunity. This means that if you miss one module exam, you may complete one make-up module exam near the end of the semester without needing to provide additional documentation for that first missed module exam. This free make-up opportunity applies to one missed module exam only and does not apply to the final exam.

Attendance, Late Work, and Make-Up Policy

Attendance is determined by completion of assigned material. Assignments, lab activities, SmartBook assignments, and quizzes are due on the dates listed in the course schedule. Most assignments include an automatic two-day extension beyond the original due date.

After the late submission deadline passes, assignments close and no additional submissions are accepted. Individual reopen requests are generally not granted.

Withdrawal, Integrity, Accessibility, and Support

Withdrawal requests after midterm are at instructor discretion and require continued course participation and at least 80% of coursework to that date completed with a grade more than 0.

Students must complete their own work. A first academic dishonesty offense results in a failing grade on the assignment. A second offense results in a failing grade for the course.
