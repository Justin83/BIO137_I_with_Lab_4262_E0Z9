BIO 137 Human Anatomy and Physiology I introduces the structure and function of the human body. This first semester includes basic chemistry, cell structure, cell physiology, metabolism, tissues, and the integumentary, skeletal, muscular, and nervous systems.

Exams and Make-Up Policy

Module Exams

  • Four module exams.
  • Each module includes a lecture exam and a lab exam.
  • Combined module value: 100 points.
  • Total module exam value: 400 points.

Final Review

  • Eight final review quizzes.
  • Worth 20 points total.
  • Designed to help prepare for the comprehensive final.

Final Exam

  • Comprehensive lecture and lab exam.
  • Covers all 12 lecture chapters and 12 lab activities.
  • Worth 100 points total.
One Free Missed Module Exam Opportunity: Each student has one free missed module exam opportunity. This means that if you miss one module exam, you may complete one make-up module exam near the end of the semester without needing to provide additional documentation for that first missed module exam. This free make-up opportunity applies to one missed module exam only and does not apply to the final exam.
6

Grade Scale

A total of 1405 points, not including bonus assignments, can be earned by the end of the semester after completing all assignments. The total points earned at the end of the semester is divided by 1405 to determine your final course grade.

A

To earn an A, students must have 1258 or more points. This is equivalent to 89.5% or above.

B

To earn a B, students must have 1117 to 1257 points. This is equivalent to 79.5 to 89.4%.

C

To earn a C, students must have 977 to 1116 points. This is equivalent to 69.5 to 79.4%.

D

To earn a D, students must have 836 to 976 points. This is equivalent to 59.5 to 69.4%.

E

To earn an E, students must earn 835 or fewer points. This is equivalent to below 59.5%.

Attendance, Late Work, and Course Progress

Attendance

Your attendance is determined by completion of assigned material. Students who do not complete required first-week attendance assignments may be dropped without notification according to home campus procedures.

Late Work

Most assignments include an automatic two-day extension period beyond the original due date. After the late submission deadline, the assignment closes.

Grade Checks

Do not rely only on Blackboard percentages. Divide points earned by points possible at that point in the course to estimate your standing.

Ask early. Use Teams, Blackboard Messages, phone, or email before a small issue becomes a course-threatening problem.

Academic Integrity and Support

Students must complete their own work. Academic dishonesty will not be tolerated. A first offense will result in a failing grade on the assignment. A second offense will result in a failing grade for the course.

Somerset Community College provides equal access to educational opportunities. Students with documented disabilities who need accommodations should contact Accessibility Services as early as possible.
